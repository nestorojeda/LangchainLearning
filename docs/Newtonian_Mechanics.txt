Introduction:
Newtonian Mechanics, also known as Classical Mechanics, is a branch of physics that explains the motion of objects under the influence of forces. Developed by Sir Isaac Newton in the 17th century, it forms the cornerstone of many physical laws used in engineering and science.

Newton's Three Laws of Motion:
1. **First Law (Law of Inertia):**
   - An object at rest remains at rest, and an object in motion continues in motion with a constant velocity unless acted upon by a net external force.
2. **Second Law (Law of Acceleration):**
   - The acceleration of an object is proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. This relationship is often expressed as F = ma.
3. **Third Law (Law of Action and Reaction):**
   - For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Applications:
Newtonian mechanics is used to model the motion of everyday objects like vehicles, projectiles, and machinery. It also provides a solid groundwork for more advanced fields of physics.

Conclusion:
Despite the advances in modern physics with theories like relativity and quantum mechanics, Newtonian mechanics remains a vital and intuitive framework for understanding the macroscopic world.
